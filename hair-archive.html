在 SMART 的视觉文化中，头发是最先被看见的部分，也是最容易被误解的部分。

它被竖起、染亮、放大、遮挡，脱离日常身体的尺度，变成一种可以被远远识别的形象。

对这些年轻人来说，发型不只是装饰。它连接着自拍、头像、QQ空间，也连接着同伴之间的识别与靠近。在工厂、出租屋和城市边缘的灰色背景中，高饱和的头发像一种临时生长出来的翅膀，让他们从重复的劳动和沉默的生活里短暂地跳出来。

这个档案库以颜色为线索整理这些发型图像：红、橙、黄、绿、青、蓝、紫。分类不是为了把他们固定成某种“奇观样本”，而是为了重新观看这些曾被嘲笑、截图、转发和遗忘的形象。

当头发被染成一种不肯融入背景的颜色，它也许正在说：

我存在，我被看见，我可以用自己的方式定义自己。

In the visual culture of SMART, hair is the first part to be seen and also the part that is most easily misunderstood.

It was erected, illuminated, magnified and obscured, breaking away from the scale of the everyday body and transforming into an image that could be recognized from afar.

For these young people, hairstyle is not merely an adornment. It links selfies, profile pictures, and QQ spaces, and also connects the recognition and approach among peers. Against the backdrop of factories, rental houses, and the gray fringes of the city, the highly saturated hair is like a pair of temporarily grown wings, allowing them to briefly break away from repetitive labor and silent life.

This archive organizes these hairstyle images by color: red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, and purple. The classification is not to fix them as some kind of “spectacle samples”, but to re-examine these images that were once ridiculed, screenshot, forwarded and forgotten.

When hair is dyed a color that refuses to blend into the background, it might be saying:

I exist, I am seen, and I can define myself in my own way.
